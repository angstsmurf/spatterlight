Prolog
Later on, you will look back upon this moment as the turning point in your life.  The fulcrum point on which the balance is swayed one direction or the other, that magical moment when everything that already has or will happen to you is divided into either occurring before or after this event.  For now, however,  you can only categorize what is happening as one of the worst ways someone can be awakened.



You try hard to focus, the sharp pain in your arm wrestling you from the deep sleep that the luxurious guest bed had been granting you till now.  Blinking, trying to force your eyes to adjust to the darkness quicker than they want to, you survey your guest room at the Domou Island Resort.  Moonlight bathes the room in a soft glow and instinctively you stare down at your left arm.  The pain is subsiding now, but a few droplets of blood can be seen speckled across your skin.  It is then, as you try to decipher exactly what has happened to you, that a tightness in your gut and something from the primal parts of your brain alert you to a terrifying truth...You are not alone in the bedroom.



The figure is tall and menacing, dressed in a black cloak with a purple velvet lining that almost touches the floor.  An overly large hood has been pulled over his head, making his face appear to be nothing but a deep, black hole.  His gloved hand holds what appears to be a syringe of some kind.  The figure regards you silently for just a moment more before bolting from the door of your bedroom with blinding speed and heading east into the foyer.  You shake off the last of the sleep, letting anger and a deep need for answers replace your fear, and leap from your bed to give chase to the intruder.

Foyer
You hurl yourself through the open door of your bedroom and into the foyer in what you hope will be enough time to get some answers from the man in black.  In the pale night light filtering in from the windows you are able to make out his shadowy outline from across the room.  You screech to a halt as he turns to face you, the foyer couch the only thing between the two of you.  Time seems to slow down, you can almost feel his muscles tense along with yours as you silently face off from only a few yards away.  And then it happens, the figure in black turns and runs for the stairway down to the lower floors of the resort.  You react quicker than you thought you were capable of, springing forward and leaping the couch but almost tripping on the edge of the rug on the other side.  Regaining your balance you sprint the final distance across the foyer to the stairwell only to witness your adversary pull down a heavy iron gate and lock it into place moments before you reach it.



For the first time since the beginning of this you come face to face with your enemy.  He is only inches from you, standing still, watching you triumphantly.  The bars of the barrier are too close together to reach your arm through or you would try to get your hands on him, gate or not.  Staring into the hood you can see nothing.  It is empty, like a deep, seething wound of the soul.  The figure nods in a curiously respectful manner and then slowly turns and walks down the stairs, leaving you alone in the foyer.



You want to yell at him to come back, to give you answers to what is going on.  But instead you are suddenly overcome with a horrible nausea.  It feels like you could lose consciousness at any moment.  You call out for help but no one seems to come to your aid.  Where is Billy the Nightkeep, Brenda, Kitty, or any of the other guests.  All that sounds good to you at this point is sleep.  You need to lie down in your bed and get a hold of yourself.  Maybe you can put this all together in the morning and get help then.
