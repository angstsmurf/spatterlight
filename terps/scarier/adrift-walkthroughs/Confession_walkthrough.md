# The Murder of Jack Morely (aka Confession) — walkthrough

- **Engine:** ADRIFT 4 (native). Mystery, ADRIFT 2nd 3-Hour Comp 2004. A
  deliberately **non-interactive** story — the author warns it's linear.
- **Result:** **Story completes** — ends `Striking a plea deal, you narrowly
  avoided the death penalty.` then re-displays the title. Solution:
  `harness/confession_solution.txt`; golden `confession_solution.expected.txt`.
- **Walkthrough source:** *Key & Compass* (native-ADRIFT).

## Derivation

There is no real interaction. In your living room the only meaningful choice is
`open door`; after that the questioning-room scene **ignores all input**
(including `quit`) and auto-advances on a timer. So the solution is the handful
of opening examines + `open door`, then `z` (wait) repeated enough times to let
the timed story play out to its fixed ending. The story length is fixed by the
events, not by input count; the game exits on its own at the end, so surplus
`z`s are harmless. The marker is the sole ending line (`Striking a plea deal`).
