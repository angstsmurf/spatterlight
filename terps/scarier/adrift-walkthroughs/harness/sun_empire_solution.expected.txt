        As chief engineer of the Rosemon Genetics Institute on Rhiannon, you,
Tangee Simone, oversee a large group of scientists working on uncovering the
genetic secrets of the Kantenese race.
        It is well known that the Kantans have a high disposition towards
tolerance of a large number of diseases.  Additionally, their culture adopted
genetic manipulation early on accepting it is an acceptable practice of their
medicine.  Few negative side-effects have ever been documented by Kantan
doctors in documentating their treatments.
        Recently through advance genome typing, it has been uncovered that
the genetic makeup of the Kantan race is a fusion of two separate and unique
genomes.  Genetic testing of the population at large has supported the theory
that the original Kantan DNA has been modified through insertion of extra DNA
from another race.
        Wild with speculation, the Kantan and Jenti scientific communities
have attributed the extraneous DNA to an ancient race simply named the
"founders".  It is said that the founders manipulated their own DNA into the
Kantan race for some purpose long lost in time.
        This has been the task to which you have worked five long years.  You
have already made a name for youself by definitively linking the founder DNA
to the exceptional flexibility of the Kantan race to undergo genetic
manipulation and withstand disease.
        Your lab contains other uncovered surprises not disclosed to the
public at large.  Within a few short months, you will be able to document
attributes certain DNA strands implement in the Kantan.  Further, your
research is beginning to link more than a single race touched by the founder
genetic material.
        Today is a Saturday.  Though the Institute is officially closed,
you've decided to come into the Institute and work.  Yesterday, your team
grew a new founder limb in the lab from a previously unstimulated section of
Kantan DNA.  You are anxious to run a comparative DNA analysis against
previous tissue samples generated...
