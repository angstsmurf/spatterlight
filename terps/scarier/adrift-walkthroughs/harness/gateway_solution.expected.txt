GATEWAY: Guardian Child
By: Michael R. Grice

Time and space flash by in blazing colours. You're dizzy and on the verge of
vomiting. Your head feels as if it is going to explode. Suddenly the flashing
lights and noise stop and the pain is gone. Your heart pounds and you breath
heavily, trying to get ahold of yourself. You realize that you're on your
back, looking up at empty blackness.

In The Gateway
You are in a dark room with a slightly transparent and glowing image of a
little girl that floats above you and slowly turns. The child has long brown
hair that hangs down the middle of her back, and her face speaks of wisdom
beyond what a girl of her apparent age should have. Stars spin slowly around
her body causing the image to pulse with a dim white light. Also in the room
is a wood framed door that leads into an expanse of stars in a black night
sky.

>You walk through the doorway...
Lights flash around you and noise zips passed. Voices grow loud and then
disappear so fast you can't tell what they are saying. Your body aches and
your head pounds as you speed through chaos. Then all is calm and your senses
slowly return to normal. You are not where you were.

Temple Courtyard
You are in a large square court yard. East of you stands the double doors to
a two story building. North can be seen a small stable where a few stable
boys silently clean and care for a couple horses. From the south the
fragrance of blooming flowers wafts into the air and in that direction you
can see a perfectly tended flowerbed. To your west towers the tall rounded
gates of the temple leading out into the Panrian Woods.
A woman cries out in terror at your sudden appearance and rushes away into
the building. A few other people in the court yard stare at you in confusion.
It appears as if an execution is about to be start.

>   Suddenly a dozen armed guards wearing shiny chain mail armour burst out of
the building to the east and charge towards you. You are weaponless and have
no way to defend yourself. The soldiers run you down before you can escape
and surround you, holding their swords and spears at the ready. One of them,
probably the leader, steps forward and glares at you suspiciously.
   Before anything else can happen, a commanding voice from above shouts
down, "Stop!" All heads, including yours, turn to a window on the second
floor of the white building. It is hard to make out any details of the man
leaning out the open window, but his white robe shows him to be a priest of
the temple. "Stop!" he shouted again. "Bring him to me!"
   The guard who had been about to address you grabs your arm and practically
drags you into the temple, hauls you up a spiralled flight of stairs, and
drops you on the floor of a bright and fresh room. You shakily stand up and
find yourself looking into the face of your rescuer. The priest smiles kindly
at you and his eyes seem to stare right through you to a time long forgotten,
or possibly a time to come.
   "Well," he starts, clapping his hands together and moving to sit in a
chair. "I have no doubt that you are confused right now. I know I would be if
our roles were reversed." The old man laughs slightly and motions for you to
sit in a chair next to him. You comply and nervously fold your hands on your
lap. "A month ago a little girl appeared in a similar fashion as yourself.
She was also sent to us by the Stars, but she was their daughter made flesh.
She was given to us so she would grow and one day become our queen. Our world
has been without a master for nearly a century, and wars for power have torn
the land apart. The Guardian Child was sent to protect the people by leading
them in peace." He studies you for a moment before continuing. "There must be
some great danger if the Stars have sent us another saviour besides the
Guardian Child. You have no idea why you are here? Hmmm, I thought not. Then
you are probably not a child of the Stars, but for whatever reason, you have
been chosen to come to our world. I would suggest that you go to the Forest
of Visions in the west and find out why the Stars sent you here. Oh," he
grabs a piece of paper and pen and quickly scrolls down a message. He hands
you the paper and says, "Deliver this to the black smith in Fayn. He will
give you supplies for your journey. Good luck."

>(Getting off the comfy chair first)
You move east.
