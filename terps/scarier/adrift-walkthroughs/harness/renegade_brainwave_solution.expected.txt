Renegade Brainwave
By J. J. Guest

An Ectocomp 2010 entry!

With a burst of eerie organ music, the ground opens up and from deep within
emerges a cobwebbed coffin. The lid creaks slowly open to reveal the pallid
form of a circus ringmaster, complete with top hat and twirly moustache.
Slowly he rises to a sitting position.

"Beware!" says the ringmaster, "Beware! Take care! For you are about take
part in an interactive story that will reveal the terrifying truth behind the
ill-fated Mexican space programme! Revelations of incredible horrors that
will terrify you with their brutal reality!"

"For this is the story of a mysterious force - a force that has crossed the
billion mile vastness of infinite space! Boneless, fleshless, almost
invisible and yet imbued with incredible power - I speak to you of cosmic
radiation!"

"The deadly solar radiation that has the power to raise the dead! The power
to amplify brainwaves to many times their natural strength - so that even a
weak little puppy could wipe out a city of concrete and steel - with the
power of his

mind! In a future that grows ever closer, the fate of the Earth lies in the
hands of one man. That man is Detective Frank Douglas; that man my friend, is
you!"

With that the Ringmaster folds his hands across his chest and lies back in
his coffin, which, with a slight wobble, descends back into the swirling mist
to be seen no more!

Cemetary Entrance
You stand at the entrance to the Restview Cemetary for Circus Folk, where a
mysterious object from space was seen to crash-land earlier tonight. The
cemetary was built rather hurridly in the middle of a fetid, stinking bog
after that evil circus came to town and tried to hypnotise and enslave all
the townspeople. From between the trees and bushes, obelisks, urns and angels
peer out at drunken angles, ivy covered tombs and headstones lie abandoned
and to the north are the moss-grown ruins of some ancient chapel, glowing
with an eerie, greenish light! To the south an open gate leads back to the
road and the safety of your patrol car. East and west, paths meander into the
gloom.
Officer Donald McRonald stands nearby, paying scant attention to what is
going on.

>You open the Mary Quant handbag.  A gun and a seventeen-foot boa constrictor
are inside the Mary Quant handbag.

Now, how on Earth did that get in there? Probably a practical joke - the
other cops are always ribbing you about your transvesitism.

>You drop the handbag rather hurridly, and the snake slithers away into the
undergrowth.

>You take the gun from the Mary Quant handbag.

>You move west.
Yew tree
You stand under the spreading shadow of a vast yew tree, gnarled and twisted
with age and knobbled with bulbous fungoid protusions. Hundreds of ragged
crows wheel above the old tree. To the east a meandering pathway disappear
into the gloom.

Also here is a crowbar.

As you near the yew tree you experience that morbid quickening of the senses
that presages a visitation from beyond the veil. Directly ahead of you,
illuminated by a ray of moonshine amid the verdurous gloom, a large gorilla
is emerging from an open grave. With a prehensile roar he lumbers off into
the darkness, disappearing among the winding, mossy ways.

"You know, Donald," you say, "I don't think that's the last we've seen of
that gorilla." But Donald is busy cleaning out his ears with a Q-Tip and
doesn't seem to have noticed the spooky goings-on. With a sigh you turn back
to the scene of your psychic visitation, and notice something glinting in the
moonlight.

Donald McRonald follows you from the east.

>You pick up the crowbar.

>You move east.

Outside the chapel
Here, beneath a shroud of ivy, lurks the ruined chapel. The chapel has been

From a gigantic hole in the roof comes an eerie light that casts a green pall

Donald McRonald follows you from the south.

>You move west.
Cemetary
This is a shady corner of the cemetary. There are a fair scattering of
variously sized headstones to be seen, including a gigantic stone angel,
alabaster white and entwined all around by the roots of some twisting tree.
To the south is the yew tree, and to the east is the ruined chapel with its
eerie green glow.
A four-hundred pound gorilla leans nonchalently against a tombstone.

Donald McRonald follows you from the east.
The gorilla sees you arrive with gun in hand and immediately throws up his
arms.

"Hey, buddy, let's not be too hasty,' he says, 'here, have a cigar!"

He offers you a cigar from his monogrammed cigar case. Which is especially
nice because that boa constrictor made off with your cigarettes.
"C'mon," says the gorilla, waggling the cigar, "I'll bet you're gasping for a
smoke!"

>"Don't mind if I do," you say. Very decent of the gorilla to offer you a
cigar - especially since that boa constrictor made off with your cigarettes.
The gorilla sniggers as he hands it over, and then slopes off into the
darkness whistling a merry tune.

The Vault of Mandoo the Mystic
Here lies the ivy-covered vault of Mandoo the Mystic, an ornate stone
structure in the style of an Indian temple that is sinking slowly into the
depths of the bog. The lead-lined door hangs open.
A horrible creature stands guard outside the vault. Four feet in height it is
and life-size only as to its enamelled head - a grinning cloth and
papier-mache doll dressed in a velveteen suit and white patent-leather
cuban-heeled shoes. A ventriloquist's dummy, brought to a shocking semblance
of life by the mysterious space radiation!

From the darkness of the vault emerges a terrible creature, knife in hand.
Four feet in height it is and life-size only as to its enamelled head - a
grinning  cloth and papier-mache doll dressed in a velveteen suit and white
patent-leather cuban-heeled shoes. A ventriloquist's dummy, brought to a
shocking semblance of life by the mysterious space radiation!

Donald McRonald follows you from the west.

>"Don't mind if I do," says the ventriloquist's dummy, though you're quite
sure you saw his lips move. Donald lights the cigar for him and you both
retreat to a safe distance. A couple of puffs later there is a VERY LOUD
EXPLOSION which sends you both temporalily deaf and takes the dummy
permanently out of commission. In fact there is nothing left of him but a
pair of white patent leather cuban-heeled shoes, size 2.

>You move in.
Inside the Vault
This is the vault of Mandoo the Mystic - the evil psychic and hypnotist who
once tried to turn the townsfolk into his willing slaves! Heavy drapes cover
the slimy walls, and guttering candles festooned with cobwebs burn dimly
illuminating the open sarcophagus. In the sarcophagus lies the be-turbaned
skeleton of the evil Mandoo!

Donald McRonald follows you from outside.

>You take the femur bone from the stone sarcophagus.

>You move in.
The Chapel
The chapel has been completely wrecked by the object that fell from the sky.
From a massive hole in the floor comes a greenish glow. Crumbling stone steps
lead down into the crypt, and a gothic archway leads back out into the
cemetary.

Donald McRonald follows you from outside.

>You move down.
The Crypt
A dismal crypt with vaulted ceiling, now largely destroyed. Sitting in the
middle of the crypt are the unmistakable remains of a Mexican Space Module.
The Mexicans have been sending these things up for years, at a cost of
billions of Pesos, but nobody quite knows why. Presumably they are out there
colonising every planet in the solar system. The door to the module has been
blown off its hinges by the impact, an eerie green glow issuing from within.
Crumbling stone steps lead up into the chapel.

Donald McRonald follows you from above.

>You move in.
Inside the space module
The inside of the space module is a mass of hissing tubes and fizzing cables.
Everything is broken, and everything is bathed in an eerie glow from a
shimmering curtain of light to the east. Clearly the impact of the crash
combined with the cosmic radiation absorbed by the module has opened up a
rift between the dimensions.

You wonder how you know all this stuff, but it is obviously the powerful
atmosphere of cosmic background information.
Sitting motionless in the flight seat is a space-suited astronaut, his
features obscured by his mirrored helmet.

Donald McRonald follows you from outside.

>You hurl the bone into the shimmering veil of light, whereupon it is swiftly
followed by one salivating chihuahua. As he enters the cosmic curtain there
is a flash of light. As if a switch had been turned, as if an eye had been
blinked, as if some phantom force in the universe had made a move eons beyond
our comprehension, suddenly, there is no veil! There is no bone, and there is
no hyper-intelligent chihuahua!

Case in point: The line between science fiction and science fact is
microscopically thin - and so is the Dread Domingo. In pursuit of his prize
he has unwittingly stepped into the second dimension - becoming as flat as a
pancake! As three pancakes! With maple syrup! And once that line has been
crossed - it can never be recrossed! The Dread Domingo is doomed to spend the
rest of his days as a mere cipher, like the denizens of Plato's cave or the
characters on a movie screen - a projection on a wall, to be glimpsed on
moonless nights by star-crossed lovers taking shortcuts across the cemetary!

Thanks to you, and the intrepid, fearless Donald, the terrible threat to
planet Earth has been averted!
