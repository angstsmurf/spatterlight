You stand in a small clearing made in the Oregon forest.  There's a small
dwelling here that looks like a hunting cabin common to this area.  You feel
like you're being watched.
There's a soldier hidden in some foliage here.

>The sentry, approaching you cooly and calmy, grips the barrel of his rifle
and comes at you swinging the butt of the gun.

You're prepared for the onslaught and manage to painfully deflect the first
swing.  Unfortunately, you lost your gun deflecting the first blow, so you
revert to your academy hand-to-hand combat training.  You knock the gun from
the sentry's hands when you drop to the ground and knock his legs out from
under him.

Both on the ground, you scramble to deliver a knock out blow but he beats you
to it and you hear the crack your nose makes as the sentry breaks it with the
barrel of a pistol he produces from a sleeve.  You struggle for but lose
consciousness.

You wake up in a nicely furnished guest bedroom.  You are lying on a soft bed
and there is cotton wadding up your nose.  You have a migrane that is
disorienting you.    There's also a taped bandage around your nose that is
tickling you.  You begin to sit up but someone pushes you firmly back down
against the bed.

You open your eyes.  Your vision is blurred at first, but begins to clear
immediately.  There are two people standing beside your bed, looking down
upon you.  One is a man, and one is a woman.  As your vision clears a little
more, you are stunned to see the elusive figures you have been searching for
the majority of your life.  Your voice sounds raspy and congested.  "Am I
dead?" you ask.  Your mother is hardly containing tears, you're not sure if
they are of sorrow or joy.  "No, William, you're not dead."  She sits down on
the bed beside you.

Your mind is overloaded with questions and thoughts.  Just now, you are
content to study them.  Your mother is older now than she was the last time
you saw her, but not remarkably changed.  However, you are enamored with your
father.  It is as if you were able to open a portal and see what you would
look like in forty years.  He looks tired and worn with endurance, but smiles
at you.  It's a rare smile.  You're gaping.  "Hello, Will" he says, awed and
nervous at the same time.  "Hi," you manage after some time.  Your mother is
looking back and forth between the two of you.  "William," she says, bursting
with emotion,  "Meet your father, Fox Mulder."  He looks down.  "I, uh, I'm
sorry about . . . so many things."  You stop him.  "Dad," you say, having
wanted to call someone that all your life.  You wait until you get his
attention, "It's okay.  You'll make me understand."  He looks back up at you,
surprised that you're not angry at him.  He searches your face for resentment
and finds none.

"There's so much we need to explain to you, son," your mother says.  Frohike
comes to mind.  "There were guys with me, four of them, what --"  "They're
upstairs, they're all okay, they're old friends of ours," your mother tells
you.  "They're much safer with us now.  I wish I could say the same about
you."  You look to your mother to explain and turn to your father when she
doesn't.  He meets your gaze.  "You're a threat to them, Will, because of
what you are --"  "Exactly what am I?  I keep hearing that, but no one tells
me."  "You're naturally immune to their virus, Will, the one they plan to use
for colonization.  You were born that way.  Because your mother and I had
both been experimented on, vaccinated against the viruses ourselves.  You're
naturally immune, though."  He paused, allowing it to sink in.  "Each of us
is born with dormant DNA in our genetic makeup, extra material that seemingly
has no place.  Your dormant DNA  can be activated and that gives you some
dangerous potential to them.  We're not even sure what you're capable of."
You stare at him speechless.  "Upon encountering an alien artifact during a
case, I began experiencing headaches.  They got worse and worse.  Very
quickly, I had developed telepathic abilities.  I had also become deathly ill
and unable to communicate.  The dormant DNA I mentioned, had become
activated, but at such a frantic frenzy, that it was killing my body.
Cancerman was after what I had and had surgery performed to remove part of my
brain.  We're not sure what really took place.  Since, I have experienced
problems related to the procedure and what took place."  You notice your
mother grip his hand a little tighter.  "It was very important that we
protect you from the people who might do the same to you.  But that's blown
and they've located us now."   You are stunned.   "I led them straight to
you."  You feel guilt and remorse.  "Not willingly, Will," your mother says.
"They didn't give you a choice, and they would have killed you if you
didn't."

"What now?" you ask.  "Stay.  Train with us.  Help the Resistence the way
only someone like you can.  You may save us all if you do.  I'd like to tell
you that you could go back if you'd like, but you can't.  They'll kill you."
Your mother looks at you.  "Do you believe in fate?" she says.  "Only when I
want to," you say.

>"I'm not sure how I should feel saying this, Nathan, to be glad you've grown
to be such a strong and smart young man, or to be sad that I can't protect
you anymore.  Either way," your father says as he extends his hand to you,
"Welcome to the Resistance."  You smile without knowing why as you grip it in
return.
