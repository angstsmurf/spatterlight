Yak Shaving for Kicks and Giggles!
Interactive Short fiction by J. J. Guest

Written with Adrift 4.00 Release 50
Part of 'The Odd Competition' organised by Abbi Park, 2008

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

yak shaving > noun (uncountable)

   1. (idiomatic) Any apparently useless activity which, by allowing you to
overcome intermediate difficulties, allows you to solve a larger problem.

This is Steve Goodwin. He's an regular guy, young, successful, dynamic.
Recently promoted to the post of junior marketing executive at the Funhouse
Novelty Company, he's the one who comes up with all those zany items you can
buy for a Dollar in the pages of Cosmic Comics.

But something is missing in Steve's life. A nagging question gnaws at his
soul, undermines his joy at his new-found success and interferes with the
very business of living. But this is not a answer he can find in the pages of
any book; for what Steve yearns to know is the very meaning of life itself!

But then he learned about a man, a guru of great wisdom who could teach him
the secrets of the universe. A six hundred year old hermit living at the top
of a mountain in a kingdom most right-thinking people assume to be a myth. A
man known as the Dada Lama!

And this is why we find him now, after many months of journeying, of
fruitless searching, beaten and battered by the uncaring elements, in this
mysterious valley hidden deep within the mountains...

...the mystical valley of Shangri-La!
