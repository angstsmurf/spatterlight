The Evil Chicken of Doom 3D

They say horror is having to stare into your own face as its being ripped off by something sharp. Or at least that's how dictionaries define it. But true horror isn't about a face being ripped off or the act of staring, it's about something dark growing in the pit of your soul, a fear that keeps eating and getting bigger and bigger until it envelops you and suddenly you're more terror than human. Also it's scary.

You and Steve had heard about the farm from an old man in the tavern. You said you were looking for a place you could hide low, to keep away from the military police who were after you for dodging the draft, and he replied by talking. He told you about an old farmyard that had been closed down before the war after its owner went crazy and murdered his own family with an axe. At first you assumed he had murdered them in self-defense, which would've been perfectly reasonable, but the old man continued to explain how the townsfolk believed an ancient supernatural chicken had enslaved his mind and forced him to do his evil bidding. "Yeah right," you said to him, "the idea of some sort of Evil Chicken of Doom roaming around enslaving people's minds sounds like the plot of some sort of highly creative and deeply terrifying video game, not something you might come across in real life!!!!"

You and Steve spit in the old man's face and ran through the night to the farm, dodging the military helicopters and speedboats that lept across the countryside searching for you. It was night by the time you reached the farm and all was quiet, too quiet, an ominous silence hushing the wind and insects from making a sound. You waited until night to make a break for the chicken coop so the darkness would mask your very loud and obvious movements and you managed to get inside the shelter making only a few alarming screams to give away your position.

"Oh.

My.

God.

Does that chicken have two noses?"

You looked to where Steve was pointed and your mouth drooped in shock. It was a chicken. It had two noses. It was clearly evil and clearly had evil magical powers.

"We need to do something about it," you replied. "And then maybe go back to that tavern and spit on that old man some more for not telling us that there was an evil chicken here!"

"I know! Let's throw a brick at it!"

"No, no. That wouldn't work..we'd have to do something fiendishly clever, something he wouldn't expect because if we just start hucking things at him, he might use his evil powers to kill us."

"Yeah, you're right...so maybe we should throw two bricks at him?"

"You know what Steve, maybe I'll find a way to kill the chicken."

"Yeah, I'll stay here and look for a brick."
