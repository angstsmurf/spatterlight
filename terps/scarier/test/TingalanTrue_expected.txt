ENCOUNTER: THE VILLAGE

You have lived in this city your whole life. You know every part of it. It looks like a different place by night: Shop windows are as lonely and black as the eye-sockets on a skull. Doorframes seem taller, the doors behind them less inviting. Merch-by-Night is not Merch-by-Day. There is very little to find here, and what may hide in Merch-by-Night may be decidedly unpleasant. Still, if you wish to search the village, you can.

> visit the wagon

You recall that, this time of year, there are often wagons hitched near the river. The strange folk who come and go along the forest roads often know something of the wood, and they may be willing to talk to you this night.

You head west, to the clearing near the bridge.

There is only one wagon, gaily painted, with strange signs carved onto the planks at odd places. It is possible they are witch-warding signs, but they do not look like any signs you have seen before.

There is a candle burning somewhere in the wagon, and a figure at work in the flickering shadows. It seems to be dancing.

As you attempt to circle the wagon and find a way in, the light in the wagon goes out...

...and the dancer stands before you, as if by magic!

He stands slightly taller than you, with a wide-brimmed hat that obscures most of his face except a very, very broad smile. His teeth are immaculate, and he seems friendly, but makes no attempt to greet you.

This is clearly a spirit, one of the true lords of Tingalan. You have read about them in books. You know they can make powerful friends or terrible enemies, and regardless of their nature, it is always wise to greet them properly.

...and so you do. You trace the sign of Ysonde in the air, and the spirit does as well. In the distance, an owl hoots with unnatural delight.

ENCOUNTER: THE SMILING SPIRIT

It speaks. Its voice is low and dark and echoes evilly among the trees. You do not think it is a friendly spirit.

"But of course, few spirits in Tingalan are friendly." It says!

"That is right, you share your thoughts with me. In this proximity, the contours of your mind are as familiar to me as the streets of this little town are to you."

Somehow, its grin grows even wider as it explains. "You are learning that this village is a different place by night. Yes. Wonderful. I am the Smiling Spirit, charged by the Fey Lord Derzelas with monitoring trade and mortal intrigues in this little patch of transient light. Your village has some small part to play in his dark machinations. You might as well...he has tasked me to make a proposition:

You leave this night hunting for treasure, most likely, but know that there are things more valuable than treasure among the trees...for the trees also guard terrible secrets. My lord would dearly like for you to learn one. He offers you this reward if you swear, on all that is holy and unholy, to return to Merch with a terrible secret."

The spirit holds out its hand. By the light of the moon, you see that its hand has no fingernails. In the palm of its hand, it holds a large black pearl. This would make you quite rich, if you were able to exchange it in the markets of Merch in the morning.

"A very generous offer." The spirit says. "accept it, and you swear to learn a terrible secret before sunrise. What say you, mortal?"

LEAVE AT ONCE

> accept

You accept the spirit's offer as politely as you can.

It upends its hand into yours. The black pearl is heavier than you expected. You have taken on quite a burden.

"If you find my master, in one of the places where the trees howl and hollow, I am sure he would be pleased to teach you a terrible secret himself." The spirit says.

It bows politely, and returns to its wagon.

You have struck a dark bargain with a power beyond your comprehension. It is time to carry it out.

Stowing the pearl in your back pocket, you set out to find a terrible secret among the trees.

> look

You are in Merch, a peaceful trading town by a river. Forests surround Merch for leagues and leagues, so far that no Man of Merch knows where the forest ends. By day, Merch is pleasant and bright, but Merch-by-Night is a different city entirely. The more mystically inclined Men of Merch say that night here lasts a hundred years. It is still night. Perhaps they are right.

You can SEARCH here, but you may not like what you find.

Here, and only here, the wood is safe enough to WAIT UNTIL DAWN.
There is a fire burning here. You can read by its light. You might even be able to cook something.

The gutters along the side of the road are overgrown with weeds and brambles. The road continues to the East, and back West to Merch itself.

ENCOUNTER: THE MEMORY

Something here reminds you of a memory from your youth...yes...it's coming back now, over distant years. God it has been so long...

REMEMBER
TRY TO FORGET (Courage)

> remember

The night was bitter cold, perhaps colder than it is now.
But you did not suffer that night...you relished the cold.
You were perhaps seven or eight years old.

Your father had prepared an iron tub, placed near the porch of your house, and heated by a pit of low coals below it...a bath of hot water, but out of doors, in the snow.

You would leap out of the tub, and charge barely clothed into the snow. The cold was a shock to be sure. You screamed and shook as the snow sapped the warmth from your skin. Then, shivering, you would climb back into the tub, and feel the sweet warmth of the water flow through you. It was pure bliss, surely the best feeling in the world. You soaked, watching the stars through the steam rising from the tub. After a few moments, you leapt back into the snow once more.

The night is growing colder by the hour, but you see now that warmth means nothing without cold. It is entirely possible you will freeze out here, but maybe, just maybe, you will return home at the end of this terrible night...and there will be low coals, and hot water, and joyful warmth once more. You can nearly feel it now.

Your courage has increased!

A light dies at the edge of your vision. You know that the watchfires of Merch have gone out. Will you ever see them again?

> remember
The air was dry and close, as it was now. Twigs snapped underfoot, grass rustled, and against the advice of nearly everyone, you and your grandfather resolved to camp on the banks of the river near Merch.

It was there, in a circle of stones and rotten logs, that you built your first fire. You pulled up rattling leaves and pine needles, twigs and branches. Your grandfather dutifully separated what would burn from what wouldn't, and taught you a thing or two about tree sap and birch bark. You built the entire fire around a single pussywillow head, which nearly went out. Your grandfather carefully tended to the failing flames, and your fire was saved.

That night, you learned many stories:

Your grandfather told you of the trickster Daelio, the only person in the whole of creation who sold his soul to the devil and somehow got it back.

He told you the three perplexing riddles of Arktos, and their ultimate (and simple!) solution.

He told you the Red Legend of Alkandar and the Green Legend of Lunakeid, and revealed that they were actually the same story told from two different perspectives.

He told you the story of how the pommelstone on Aenarleoch's sword was lost, and how the great hero Lugarsphi got it back again.

He told you a little, only a little, about the Night that existed beyond this world.

He told you that to the night, your life was nothing special...but here, now, in this world, your life was quite special indeed.

You have built many fires since then, but you swear that fire burned hotter and brighter than any you have built since. Perhaps one day you will build a fire like that again.

Your wits have increased!
Your lore has increased!
Your studies have been strangely fruitful...you think you can cast a new spell.

The spell you have learned is the SIGN OF DANTOR. This consumes a stalk of stinkhorn, and curses an enemy in combat.

> remember
It was a cold winter night, just like this one.

You could see your breath like a vengeful ghost on the night wind. The sun had just set, and you were far from home. You, and your cousin, were willing to try almost anything to get home before the darkness took you.

And so, despite the fact that the first frost had fallen only a few weeks ago, you resolved the cross the river near Merch. The river was covered by ice, but you knew it would be quite thin.

Your cousin was a brilliant fellow, and taught you a few tricks as night fell: you were to carry a stout stick, and proceed slowly, using your staff as a third leg. At the first sign of creaking ice, you were to slowly drop, and spread your weight out as widely as possible.

And it worked! At least at first. You nearly made it across, though the ice groaned like a primeval beast and the wind picked up and knocked mighty branches from the trees.

Perhaps ten feet from the river bank, the ice gave way underfoot, much too quickly for you to react. You fell through the ice. The strange thing was: you do not remember fear. You do not even remember cold, for it set on so suddenly and so quickly that it was instantly a part of you. Color and light faded from your vision, and you figured yourself for dead, until you realized you still held your stick firmly in your hands. Holding it from the freezing water, you relied on your cousin to lift you up. Your next memory is huddling around a fire, back at the family home.

You learned an important lesson that day: a little preparation can go a long way.
You learned something else: dying might not be so bad, after all.

Perhaps these lessons will help you survive another cold night in Tingalan.

Your wits have increased!
Your courage has increased!
