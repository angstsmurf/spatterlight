The Euripides Enigma

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game may not be for you.

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

Start Options:

If you have already started the game and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the blue folder icon above.

To Begin the game from the first location, press B and then Enter.

To read the prologue and playing instructions (highly recommended if you  are starting this game for the first time) press P and then Enter.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not yet support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK.

If you are visually impaired you can increase or decrease the font size whilst playing this game by holding down the CTRL key and moving your mouse wheel.

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab  above.

In Main Airlock
You are in the main airlock of Euripides Base. The outer door has been half torn off its hinges allowing you to go Out, but the inner door is closed. On the wall is a simple control panel with just one large button.  Lieutenant Zen, Sergeant Boone, Corporal Jones, Lance-Corporal Davey and Private Foley are here.

Lieutenant Zen examines the control panel, presses the green button and then turns to you.

"Maybe if we could jury-rig the control panel then we might be able to get the inner door open?"

You think for a moment and consider three things to order him to do:

1: "Fire your pulse rifle at the inner door."
2: "Fire your laser pistol at the inner door."
3: "Jury-rig the inner door so that it will open."

> 3
You tell Lieutenant Zen to jury-rig the control panel.

The lieutenant nods and smiles. "Certainly Captain," he says, sketching a quick salute.

He turns to Lance-Corporal Davey. "OK Kelly, do your stuff!"

Lance-Corporal Davey attaches her pulse rifle to her space armour and pulls out a small toolkit. She unscrews the control panel, revealing the circuitry behind it. Switching over a couple of cables and doing a few other things you cannot see, she then screws the panel back in place and, putting her toolkit away, turns to face you.

"There ya go, Skipper," she says in her cockney accent. "The door'll open now," she tells you. "We can close it once we're on the other side, but it won't open again."

> press green
You press the button and after a few seconds the inner airlock door slides open.

Lieutenant Zen tests the atmosphere inside the base.

"Seems like the breathable atmosphere inside has leaked away, sir, probably because the controls have been damaged," he informs you. "We'll have to find the atmospheric controls, fix them and switch them on again."
