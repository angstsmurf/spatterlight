Finn's Big Adventure

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it. The game is also quite long.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game may not be for you.

I do hope, however, that you will give it a try!
There are hints and a walkthrough is available on request (type HELP).

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

Start Options:

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not yet support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK.

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab above.

THE KEY!!!!

You wonder to yourself what all that means, but a sixth sense (inherited from your father) tells you that if Magor has taken the time to write it down then it must be important! Maybe you should copy it onto something?

> x books

> write the key on the paper
You take Magor's quill, dip it into the ink pot and, in your best handwriting (Ha!), you copy the words of 'The Key' as carefully as you can onto the scrap of paper, putting the quill back on the desk when you have finished. Wow, you didn't make any blots!

> read thin book
You skip through the book, reading the most interesting bits.

Some of the words are long and you don't know what they mean. Near the end of the book is the most interesting bit of all: it mentions that there are thought to be vast catacombs under the castle but it is not known how to find the entrance.

Wow, these catacombs sound real cool, you just gotta find how to get into them! You think for a moment - if they are UNDER the castle then maybe you get to them from the deepest part of the castle? Where's that? The DUNGEONS!!

You'd better go to your rooms and change outta these clothes. If you were to get them dirty while exploring then Mom would be very angry, and you don't wanna do that coz she'll just ground you, as usual.

You replace the book on the shelf.

You are in the main room of your family's chambers. The door to the corridor is to the south and you can go Out that way, your own room is to the west which you can go In to. Your Room
You are in your room in your family chambers within Domreil Castle. There is a chest at the foot of your bed and a square table sits opposite with a chair beside it. There is a fireplace in the west wall, but there is no fire burning in it, while to the East is the door to the main room. Affixed to the wall above the fireplace is a metal sconce in which there is a resin torch that is shining brightly. On the chair next to the table you see your canvas rucksack and there's something on the table.

You rest on your bed while you wait for your mother to clear up after dinner - you would help her but you have an unfortunate habit of dropping plates. You take off your shoes and throw them under your bed (like everything else!), take off your clothes and toss them into the wicker basket (to please your mother...for a change) then get into bed. You lay there for a while, planning how you are going to sneak out after your mother has gone to sleep and make your way down to the dungeons in order to start your search for these catacombs.

After an hour or so, your mother comes in to say goodnight She tucks you in (Pl-ease! I'm not a baby any more!) gives you a kiss on the cheek (urgh!) and, turning off the resin torch with the magic words "Tasch Losch", leaves you to go to her own room. As she goes out the door she spots your discarded clothes in the basket.

"Good God," she exclaims. "You put your clothes in the basket!" She gives you a suspicious look. "Are you after something?" she asks with a smile on her face. "Who, me?!" you say in mock horror as she shuts the door.

You wait for an hour to make sure she is asleep and then you get up, making the resin torch flare into life as you do with a quiet "Tasch zunde". You'll have to hurry as your father is expected home tonight!
