The Spectre of Castle Coris

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game might not be for you.

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

THE SPECTRE OF CASTLE CORIS - Start Options:

Press B (enter) to Begin the Adventure from the First Location.

Press P (enter) to read the Prologue and Playing Instructions.
(Recommended if starting this adventure for the first time)

If you have already started the adventure and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the Blue Folder Icon in the taskbar above.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned by the author as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK and the output colour to CYAN.

If you are visually impaired you can increase or decrease the font size whilst playing this game by holding down the CTRL key and moving your mouse wheel.

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab  above.

East of Corwyn
You're on the road to Corwyn, about a mile east of the town. The walk from the farm where you left your horse, which is about four miles back east, has left you footsore and thirsty. Looking west, you see the town, with Castle Coris dimly visible on the hill beyond the town. By the side of the road is a milestone.

> w
As you walk towards Corwyn, you meet some men, women & children, laden with what appears to be all of their worldly goods, heading east. You stop to speak with them, and you realise that they are very frightened.

"If you've any sense, you'll turn around and go straight back to where you've just come from!" one of the men tells you. "We've decided to leave before the Spectre visits us!" a young woman calls out as she hurries past.

You try to ask them about this 'spectre', but they are too set on getting as much distance between themselves and the town as they hurry east along the road. You head west towards Corwyn, determined to get to the bottom of this 'spectre' business.

Corwyn Town Square
You're in the main square of the small town of Corwyn. The damp, rubbish-strewn cobblestones are empty of life, and a stone war memorial stands forlornly in the middle. Surrounding the square are houses and shops, most of which are empty or boarded up. The wide road leads East and continues West from the square. Narrower streets wind off North and South.

As you stand looking around at the deserted town square, you feel the crawling sensation in your back that warns you that somebody is watching you.

Sure enough, you see a man staring at you from the building at the corner of the street that leads north from the square. When he sees that you have spotted him, the man turns and walks away along the street. You see him enter a building further along the street on the west side.

In Tavern
You are inside the tavern, which is a large room full of tables, chairs and benches with a counter at one end. Every available seat is taken by one of the townspeople, who have gathered here for safety. Behind the counter stands the large figure of the tavernkeeper and behind him are several casks above which is a single shelf with various bottles arrayed along it. The door leads East to the street outside.

As you enter the tavern you see that it is full of frightened-looking townspeople talking amongst themselves. The hubbub of noise goes quiet as you walk in as everyone's eyes turn towards you. The man who was watching you in the town square points to you and mumbles something to the people surrounding him.

You walk up to the counter behind which stands the landlord, who regards you with suspicion, but his voice is not entirely unfriendly when he speaks.

"Good day to you sir, what can I get you?" he asks you.

> buy ale
You tell the landlord that you will have some ale, placing a gold coin from your pouch onto the counter.

As he is drawing some ale into a tankard from one of the casks, you tell him who you are. There are gasps of surprise from the townspeople and Bernie sighs with relief.

"Forgive me, your grace, I didn't know who you were," the tavern keeper says apologetically to you, as he hands you the tankard. "Have you come to deal with the spectre?"

You tell everyone that you have indeed come to deal with it and you ask Bernie to tell you about this spectre.

"It all started when Baron Coris returned from hunting down the Xixon invaders with the King and yourself," the tavernkeeper tells you. "He seemed alright at first, but a short while after he went all funny and shut himself away in the castle. Then old Percy, the groundsman, told us he heard screams one night, and after, Baron Coris told him that Baroness Coris had left to visit relatives. Percy reckons Baron Coris murdered his poor wife, and buried her body somewhere in the castle!"

He pauses in his story and pours himself a stiff drink. After downing it in one gulp, he continues.

"Then the spectre appeared one night in the church - right in the middle of evensong! We couldn't get outta that church fast enough! A few of us stopped outside when we heard poor old Reverend Rumsey scream - we thought he was following us - but by the time we got back inside, the poor old soul was dead!"

He stops again and has another drink.

"That were the start of it. Three people have been killed so far, and the other day Bran Foscar's fiancée Megan was spirited away by the spectre. We found that if you carry a bible or a prayer book and read the Lord's Prayer out loud, the spectre will disappear. Nevertheless, folk whose families have lived here for generations just upped and left. Most of us who are still here have nowhere else to go, otherwise we'd be off as well. Most folk are trying to carry on as normal, but we never go anywhere without a prayer book!"

You look round and see the townsfolk all holding up a prayer book to show you. The tavernkeeper stops his narrative and serves some of his customers, leaving you to ponder on his words.
