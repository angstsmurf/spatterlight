The Dwarf of Direwood Forest

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game may not be for you.

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

Please type the letter O then press Enter to Continue to the Start Options Page.

> o
THE DWARF OF DIREWOOD FOREST

Start Options:

If you have already started the game and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the blue folder icon above.

To Begin the game from the first location, press B and then Enter.

You are highly advised to read the prologue and playing instructions, especially if you are starting this game for the first time. To do this press P and then Enter. You should also view the VOCAB list as there are a number of non-standard verbs used in this game.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned by the author as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK and the output colour to CYAN.
You can adjust these colours by clicking on VIEW in the taskbar above and selecting "Options"

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab  above.

DISCLAIMER:
Please be advised that this game contains some content which some players might find offensive.

Forest Road
You are on a North-South road in Direwood Forest, near the border with the Turdrock Clan lands, which are further south. Thick, impenetrable forest lies to east and west, the trees clustered closely together, their roots hidden amongst the tangled vegetation of the forest floor.

> i
You are wearing a leather jerkin, a red kilt, a war belt, a battle axe and a pair of boots, and are carrying nothing.  Attached to your belt is a pouch and a sheath in which sits your knife.

> x me
You are a dwarf of clan Wyldstone, about 4'6" in height with long auburn hair and a big bushy beard.

> x beard
Your beard, which hangs down to below your waist, is very bushy and quite dense. You've found it useful for hiding small objects in from time to time. You keep it bound up to stop it getting in your way.

> s
You move South.

Forest Road
You are on the forest road where, to the East, you see a trail leading through the tangled undergrowth between the trees.

As you walk along, keeping a lookout ahead for any trouble approaching, you hear the sound of birds squabbling coming from the east.

> e
You move East.

West Bank of Stream
You stand on the west bank of a shallow stream that trickles from north to south, parallel to the path that you were walking along.

You are startled by the loud flutter of wings as several large birds take off from the far bank of the stream. You notice they are mainly scavengers and are horrified to see that they had been feeding on the dead body of a dwarf. One bird, however - a huge dire vulture - remains standing on the chest of the body, staring at you defiantly with fierce yellow eyes.

West Bank of Stream
You stand on the west bank of a shallow stream that trickles from north to south, parallel to the path that you were walking along.  On the far bank lies the body of a dwarf which has a dire vulture standing on its chest.

> x bank

> get rock

> throw rock at vulture
You throw the rock as hard as you can at the dire vulture.

As luck would have it, the rock hits the bird's left eye and the vulture screeches in shock. Shaking its head in pain it takes to the air with powerful beats of its wings and disappears from view beyond the treetops of the surrounding forest.

Whyldstone Border
You are on the road through the forest, at the border of your own clan's territory. This is marked by two totems on either side of the path. Lounging on either side of the path are three border guards, your clanmates Kennpedd, Kimmboon and Roosloov. The road leads NW and continues South across a short area of "No Man's Land" to the Turdrock border about 100 yards away.

Squatting and sitting here are three of your clanmates, Kimmboon, Roosloov and Kennpedd, who are on sentry duty today.

They greet you warmly and all wish you good luck on your quest. Before you carry on, you have some questions for them:

1: "Do you have any food?"
2: "Do you have anything to drink?"
3: "Have you seen any activity at the Turdrock border?"

> 1
You ask the sentries if they have any food to spare?

"Forget to bring some food with you?," Roosloov says, chuckling. "You'd forget yer head if it weren't screwed on!" he jests, making everyone laugh.

Kennpedd rummages around in a backpack and takes out a small package which he hands to you.

You thank him and Kimmboon says, "There, that'll keep you going for a few days if you take it easy."

> 2
You ask if the dwarves have they any water to spare.

"Don't tell me you left without any water!" Kimmboon exclaims, incredulously. You sheepishly admit that you did, causing the three dwarves to shake their heads in disbelief.

"Help 'im out Roos," he says to the blond-haired dwarf. Roosloov takes a leather flask from his warbelt and hands it to you.

"Whatever you do don't lose it," Roos warns you. "It belongs to me dad, it'll never go empty and the water inside is always cold  - some sort of magic spell on it I think."

You thank Roosloov and assure him that you will guard it with your life.

> 3
You ask the dwarves if they have seen any activity at the Turdrock border?

"Well, there were several guards there earlier today and then we saw someone - he looked like an officer - arrive and talk to them," Kimmboon tells you.

"They stood there arguing amongst themselves for a minute or two and then the officer bellowed at them and they all shut up," Roosloov continues.

"Next thing ya know they all troops off south along the path, leaving the border unmanned," KennPedd concludes. "They've never done that before!"

"You'd better get going before those guards come back," Roosloov tells you, and the other guards agree with him.

"Yes, I'd better, hadn't I?" you reply.

The three dwarves turn their attention to watching the border.

> s
You move South.

Turdrock Border
You are at the border of the Turdrock Clan, on a road that goes North and South through the forest. As with your clan's border, it is marked by two totems either side of the path.

As you reach the border you stop and look around. As the Whyldstone sentries had told you, the border seems to be unmanned...but you get the uneasy feeling that you are being watched.

> s
You move South.

You set off south but you have only gone a couple of yards when suddenly a weighted net is flung over you!

You trip over and fall to the ground, becoming entangled in the net's folds. As you struggle to free yourself a hard heavy object hits your head and everything goes black...  You regain consciousness as you are being frogmarched in darkness and you realise that you are deep underground. Your eyes gradually adjust to the darkness and after a few minutes you stop outside a barred cell. Your captors, dwarves of the Turdrock clan, bind your hands and, having already taken your axe, start to search you, taking anything you are carrying.

They find nothing in your pouch, much to their disgust, and so you are thrown into the cell and and the door closed with a loud echoing screech of rusty hinges behind you. A dwarf guard locks the door with another groan of metal and you are left on your own.

Cell 1
You are in a dark, dank underground cell somewhere in the Turdrock clan's territory. The walls are covered in a sheen of slimy green moisture and the floor is covered with stinking, rotting straw. The door leads Out to the tunnel beyond but it is closed and locked at the moment.

> get all from beard
Ok, you take the sheath knife, the metal key and the dire vulture droppings from out of your beard.

> x wall
The cell walls are solid rock and coated with slimy moisture. You notice in one wall, at about chest height to a dwarf, there is a narrow crack.

> put knife in crack
Holding the knife carefully by the blade you push the hilt into the crack as far as you can.

> cut rope with knife
You carefully rub the rope on the blade of your knife and within a few minutes you have sliced through it. As the remains of the rope fall to the floor you rub your wrists to restore circulation. You then take the knife out of the crack and put it back in its sheath.

> x moisture
It seem to issue from the very walls themselves, you've never seen the like before.

> x door
A door made up of thick, rusty steel bars with a lock, the hinges are also rusty and it is closed and locked.

> x hinges
The hinges of the cell door are heavily rusted.

> rub slime on hinges
You scrape some of the slimy moisture off the wall in one hand and rub it onto the hinges of the cell door. You notice it seems to dissolve the rust somewhat.

> rub key in slime
You rub the key in the slimy moisture off the wall. You notice it seems to dissolve the rust from the key and coat it with a layer of slime.

> unlock door
(using the metal key)
You insert the key into the lock of the cell door and turn it.

You do so very carefully and thankfully the slime you rubbed onto the key helps to lubricate it so that no noise is made as the tumblers fall.

> open door
You open the cell door.

Fortunately the slime you put on the hinges helps the door to open a lot more quietly.

> o
You move Out.

Underground Tunnel

Underground Tunnel
You are in a tunnel deep underground which leads East and West, by a cell similar to the one you were held in. The door is closed and locked. On the wall next to the cell door is the number 2. Just a little further to the east there is an opening leading south.

Suddenly a soldier comes out of the room to the east. You flatten yourself against the south wall of the tunnel as he strides away to the north.

"See ya soon, I'm back on guard duty," he shouts over his shoulder to someone in the room he just left as the dwarf disappears from view around a corner.

Maybe it would be a good idea to look around the corner to see who is in the room?

> look around corner
You peep around the corner of the opening and look at what is in there. You see a dwarf sitting on a chair at a table with his back to you.

> e

By Guard Room
The tunnel from the West bends around to the North here, and there is a guard room to the South.

In the room to the south, only about 6 feet from you, a guard sits at a table, his back to you. You'd better hurry up and do something before he senses your presence!

> creep south
You creep as quietly as you can into the guard room.

The Guard Room
You are in a large guard room. There are benches around the walls of the room, which is hewn from solid rock, and in the middle a wooden table. The only exit is North to the tunnel outside.

The guard hasn't realised you are behind him - you'd better act quickly!

> get knife
(from the leather sheath)
You take your knife from its sheath.

> kill dwarf with knife
Before the guard can react, you plunge your knife into his spine just under the rim of his helmet, killing him instantly. The guard slumps face-first onto the table, his helmet rolling off of his head onto the table.

The Guard Room
You are in a large guard room. There are benches around the walls of the room, which is hewn from solid rock, and in the middle a wooden table. The only exit is North to the tunnel outside.  The dead guard is slumped across the table.

> x guard
The guard bears an uncanny resemblance to you, as he has long auburn hair and a bushy beard. A sword is in a scabbard at his waist. He is slumped on the table he is seated at. The knife that killed him protrudes from his back. He is wearing a similar kilt to you except that it is coloured blue.

On top of the table you see a battle axe, some travel rations, a leather flask and a metal helmet.

> wear axe

> x badge

> search guard

> get scabbard
