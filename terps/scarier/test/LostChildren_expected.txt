The Lost Children

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game may not be for you.

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

Start Options:

If you have already started the game and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the blue folder icon above.

To Begin the game from the first location, press B and then Enter.

To read the prologue and playing instructions (highly recommended if you  are starting this game for the first time) press P and then Enter.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned by the author as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK and the output colour to CYAN.

If you are visually impaired you can increase or decrease the font size whilst playing this game by holding down the CTRL key and moving your mouse wheel.

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab  above.

You stand on a low mound in the middle of a desolate moor. To the North lies a forest, to the East a range of mountains, South is mostly woodland, while West you see a range of low, steep hills. Above you rears the megalith, and you can go In to the gateway through the glowing sheet of light.

> speech

In this adventure, you will find that some characters will speak to you first when you first meet them. You can either SAY HELLO or reply to whatever they have said to you. If you are going to use the command INTRODUCE MYSELF you must specific the name of the character you are talking to, e.g. INTRODUCE MYSELF TO ANNE.

Obtaining information from the characters can be very useful, so you should them questions, e.g. ASK BERNIE ABOUT SPECTRE, etc. On the other hand, you may need to tell them things that they need to know, for example TELL PEOPLE ABOUT SORCEROR. NOTE: Do not use a 'definite article' (e.g. "the" or "a") before any noun when questioning characters.

If you want to purchase any items you should simply input BUY ITEM. There is no need to tell the vendor you want to buy it, i.e. SAY TO PEDLAR BUY LINCTUS. You do not need to physically remove coins from whatever they are in, the appropriate number will be deducted from the total whenever you buy anything.

NOTE: If there is only ONE character present, you can simply input, e.g., ASK ABOUT WARES or just SAY HELLO. However, if there are two or more characters present (an animal or bird, etc, may count as a character), you must specify which character you wish to speak to, e.g. SAY HELLO TO BILL. This is very important - it may save your life!
