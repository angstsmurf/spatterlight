Start Options:

If you have already started the game and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the blue folder icon above.

To Begin the game from the first location, press B and then Enter.

To read the prologue and playing instructions (highly recommended if you  are starting this game for the first time) press P and then Enter.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not yet support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK.

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab above.

Remember: You can see in dark or gloomy places by typing LUMINO.

"Ah, there you are," you say to the archway. "I was wondering when you were going to put in an appearance. I need to check on my whisky production." The archway says nothing in return but manages somehow to look embarrassed.

> out

As you go to leave your chambers, there is a knock upon the door.

Opening it, you see King Kelson and Duke Alaric Blackmoon standing there, both grinning like Cheshire Cats. After they walk in, you greet them enthusiastically and invite them to sit down, which they do on the sofa, with you sitting in your armchair.

"So, did you find any Xixons?" you ask, fully expecting them to say no.

The king gestures for Duke Alaric to start and he tells you of their recent journey across the Great Sand Sea and beyond, and how they eliminated the Xixon warriors in Xanix city and the huge hybrid in the forest. You are extremely shocked to hear this.

"I never imagined that the Xixon were capable of such things," you tell them. "I wonder if they had help from anyone?"

Then Alaric tells you that he was able to invoke the power of the Axe of Kolt when Kelson was knocked out by the hybrid and he eventually decapitated the beast. Your mouth drops open at this news and you look from the king to Alaric and back again, as realisation dawns.

"Yes, indeed," Kelson says. "That means that Alaric must have Haldane blood in him and we must be related in some way."

"The problem is," Alaric chips in. "I have no knowledge whatsoever of any connection between the Blackmoon and Haldane families," he tells you.

"That's where you come in," Kelson tells you. "We want you to research my family history to find out where the connection is. Alaric wasn't born in Hecate, and that's what the puzzle is."

"Phew!" you say, very surprised, and reply, "Well, I will certainly do my best. The records in the castle archives go back many generations, I am sure I can find out something."

With that, the men leave the room, thanking you profusely, the king reminding you about his order for another case of your whisky. After they are gone you sit back in your chair and, scratching your head, you think to yourself, "Well, as the magician said: 'Izzy whizzy, let's get busy!'"

Your first step must be to go see "Stinker", the castle archivist. He lives in the castle archives, which are down in the deepest part of the castle near the dungeons.

Outside The King's Audience Chamber
You are standing in front of the doors to the King's audience chamber. Two soldiers in ceremonial uniforms stand guard outside the doors, their halberds crossed to prevent unauthorised entry.

"Your six times great-grandfather," you say, pointing at the king, "was one Severus Haldane, eighteenth king of Hecate."

"Never heard of him," the king replies. "I never was any good at my history lessons!"

Pointing at Duke Alaric, you continue.

"And your six times great grandmother was a certain Minerva Blackmoon," you say to Duke Alaric. He smiles and says, "Now that name I DO know," he tells you. "I remember my father mentioning it to me when I was a child."

"They married in the year 2525 and had three sons, one of whom was your ancestor. Therefore, by my reckoning that makes the two of you sixth cousins!"

The two men look at you then at one other and then burst into laughter. They both stand up and, having a man-hug and slapping each other on the back, exclaim, "Hello cousin!"

As the three of you discuss the ramifications of the news, the king makes an announcement.

"We must celebrate this wonderful news," he declares. "We will have three days of festivities in one week's time!"
