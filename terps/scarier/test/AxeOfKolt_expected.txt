The Axe of Kolt

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game may not be for you.

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

Please type the letter O then press Enter to Continue to the Start Options Page.

THE AXE OF KOLT  Start-Up Options:

Press B (enter) to Begin the Adventure from the First Location.

Press P (enter) to read the Prologue and Playing Instructions.
(Recommended if starting this adventure for the first time)

If you have already started the adventure and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the Blue Folder Icon in the taskbar above.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned by the author as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not support an authorial override of a player's colour preferences, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK and the font colour to CYAN.

If you are visually impaired you can increase or decrease the font size whilst playing this game by holding down the CTRL key and moving your mouse wheel.

Before starting, please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab in the taskbar above.

Crossroads
You are at a crossroad on the main highway that runs from north to south through the kingdom of Scippar. Here two other narrower roads head off to the East and West. By the side of the road is a freshly-painted signpost. The ground under your feet is covered in a layer of brown leaves which have fallen from the trees that surround you. Through the thickly clustered bare branches of the trees you can just about see, to the north, the mighty Droomdark mountains that form a spine across the continent.

You hear the haywain move off behind you, and turn to wave to the farmer, who gives you a nod and a thumbs-up for luck as he flicks his reins onto the backs of his oxen in a bid to encourage them to move a bit quicker.

By Old Church
You are in the southeast corner of the village green, standing by the door to the church, which leads South. Nearby is a gap that leads East through an iron fence into the village graveyard. The road through the village is to the North, NW or NE.

A woman comes out the church, wrapped in a cloak to ward off the cold wind. As she passes, you nod a greeting and smile. She looks at you with a slightly curious look on her face, but she returns your greeting with a nod and then hurries off towards the cottages on the north side of the green.

She is followed shortly after by several other villagers, some of whom pass you by without noticing you, but one or two glance in your direction and nod politely.

Finally, the vicar leaves the church and, shutting the door behind him, walks towards you. He is a surprisingly young man, probably in his late twenties, and as he passes you he doffs his hat and smiles. "God be with you, my friend," he says cheerily. You nod your head and reply in kind, and he walks away across the green, whistling merrily.

SE Corner of Village Green
You stand on the southeast corner of the village green. On the other side of the road, due south, lies the village church. The eastern side of the village green is hemmed in by impenetrable vegetation with forest beyond that, but you can go North, NW, West and SW from here.

A man comes towards you pushing a wheelbarrow which has some stakes in it. He nods a greeting to you and proceeds to unload the contents of the wheelbarrow, which includes a length of rope and a sledgehammer.

As you watch he hammers one of the stakes upright into the ground then measures off 5 strides from the first stake and drives another stake into the earth. He then turns 90 degrees, measures off another 5 strides and drives another stake into the ground. He repeats this a third time to make a square.

Throwing the sledgehammer into the wheelbarrow, he picks up the rope and ties it to one of the stakes and then goes around the square of stakes, looping it over the other three before tying the rope off on the stake where he started.

Finally he takes a notice and a hammer from the wheelbarrow and nails the notice to one of the stakes. Placing the hammer back into the wheelbarrow, he picks up the handles and wheels the wheelbarrow off towards the cottages to the north of the green and he disappears behind one of them.

Inn Yard
You are to the north of the inn, in a large cobbled yard. On the west side of the yard there are storage sheds and to the north the stables. The track by the village green is to the East. Here you see a brewer's dray.

You see a man, probably the innkeeper, open the doors of a storage shed and go inside. He fetches out what looks like a large thick cushion and drops it by the side of the dray. He looks around as if seeking someone.

"Jonnie K!" he yells, "where are ya, ye lazy git?!". He waits a moment or two then, realising he is not going to get any help, he climbs up onto the dray and manhandles a barrel of ale from the top of the pile and rolls it so that it falls off the dray onto the cushion with a dull thud. He then jumps down and rolls the barrel towards the storage shed.

"I'll bloody murder that lazy bugger Jonnie!" you hear him mutter as he rolls the barrel into the storage shed. "Always skiving off when there's work to be done!"
