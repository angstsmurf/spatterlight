Bug Hunt On Menelaus

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game may not be for you.

I do hope, however, that you will give it a try! There are in-game hints provided, and a walkthrough is available on request (type HELP).

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

By Shuttle
You are by your shuttle, which has landed on the outskirts of a large town. You can go South to the town or In to the shuttle.  Lieutenant Zen, Sergeant Boone, Lance-Corporal Davey, Corporal Jones and Private Foley are here.
As you and your marines disembark from the shuttle, you are met by the commander of one of the decimated marine regiments which is being withdrawn from the planet.

"There are about half a dozen of those bugs left in that town, they could be anywhere, absolutely anywhere," the Colonel tells you. "They are very quick to spray that gunk which burns like boiling water through ice," he says, indicating the deep gouges on his space armour. "We also found that the really big ones have hides that are impervious against our pulse rifles and laser pistols," he warns you.

Taking a satchel from his shoulder, he reaches inside and hands each of you a small device. "This counter records the destruction of an adult Meneltra each time one is killed if you press the green button. The Meneltra are very close to spawning so you'll only have about an hour or so to find and kill them all, if even one is left alive it can spawn thousands of larvae. We have downloaded information about the Meneltra to your in-suit computers and you can view this with the command ACCESS MENELTRA."

As you digest this information, the Colonel is summoned to the ship evacuating the remnants of his command. Before he goes, he issues one last request.

"The Aquilian authorities have asked that we keep damage to property to a minimum, as hopefully the townspeople will be returning after we have eradicated the bugs."

With that, he wishes you good luck and departs.

"Looks like we got our work cut out, skipper," Lance-Corporal Davey tells you. "Better get on with it then," Lieutenant Zen says. "We only have short time to find all these Meneltra and deal with them!"
