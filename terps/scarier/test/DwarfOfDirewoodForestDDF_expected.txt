THE DWARF OF DIREWOOD FOREST

Start Options:

If you have already started the game and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the blue folder icon above.

To Begin the game from the first location, press B and then Enter.

You are highly advised to read the prologue and playing instructions, especially if you are starting this game for the first time. To do this press P and then Enter. You should also view the VOCAB list as there are a number of non-standard verbs used in this game.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned by the author as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK and the output colour to CYAN.
You can adjust these colours by clicking on VIEW in the taskbar above and selecting "Options"

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab  above.

Please be advised that this game contains some content which some players might find offensive.

Hiding Under Tarpaulin
You are lying on the cargo bed of the wagon underneath the tarpaulin, squeezed up against the barrels that are the wagon's cargo.

You lay there not daring to move, hardly breathing, listening as the wagon driver finishes his business and walks back to the wagon. You feel the wagon lurch as he climbs up onto his seat and hear him urge the horses forward. The wagon sets off along the road and as it enters the camp you hear the hubbub of many people and soldiers around you.

The wagon makes a right turn and after a minute it is brought to a halt. You hear the driver call out, "Load of gunpowder for the arsenal!" to someone, probably the officer. You hear a stern voice demand "Papers!" followed by the rustle of paperwork being handed over.

"We were expecting two wagons, where's the other one?" the officer asks the driver.

"Da driver 'ad a problem wiv 'is wagon," the driver tells the officer. "'Eld 'im up a bit, he should be 'ere soon."

You steel yourself for the lifting of the tarpaulin and the discovery by the soldiers as they inspect the load, but - to your great relief - that doesn't happen. Instead you hear the officer order "OK, open the doors and let 'im in!"

The wagon rolls forward and the light dims as it enters a building, coming to a halt seconds later. The driver jumps down from the wagon and you hear him walk away, then there is silence.

You think to yourself that maybe you should get back to the fork in the road to await this second wagon and find out what it is carrying?
