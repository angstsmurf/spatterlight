> help
Lost Coastlines is a procedurally-generated, text-based, open-world role-playing game set in a dream that you, the player, are having.
You can interact with the world by text that you see in ALL CAPS. Additionally, this list of commands will work anywhere:

CARGO: Displays a list of what is in your ship’s cargo hold.
COMMANDS: Displays this list of commands
DIFFICULTY or ODDS: Displays a list of the summaries output by game’s difficulty calculator
EQUIP or WEAR: allows you to equip an item in your inventory
INVENTORY: Displays a list of what items you possess and are wearing
LOOK or EXAMINE (or L or X): Displays a description of your current location
LOOK [OBJECT] or EXAMINE [OBJECT] displays a description of an object in your inventory
SAVE: Saves a copy of your game. Map locations will not be preserved from one save to another.
STATUS: Displays relevant character information
UNDO: Allows you to UNDO the last action you made
UNDO LOCK (or IRONMAN): disables the undo function (warning, this can’t be undone)
WAKE UP: Ends the game, and gives you a final score

Once you finish building your character, you will be placed in a dream-world that is randomly generated by the game. You can move around the world by typing a cardinal direction or its abbreviation: NORTH or N, or Northwest or NW, and so on.

Most locations in the world will be open ocean, but when you arrive at an interesting destination, places of interest will appear... Options will be displayed in location descriptions as ALL CAPS text. At each location, you can choose to do one thing before moving on. Once you make this choice, you will not be able to do anything else at that location until the map resets (you will get an alert in yellow text when this happens).

Periodically, you will be presented with encounters, based on your choices at a particular location. These encounters will present your character with further choices, often with odds of success or failure represented in colored text. You can see the exact percentile meaning of these summaries by typing DIFFICULTY or ODDS...but the summary is pretty self explanatory. Options which are EASY have a high chance of success, Options which are DIFFICULT have a low chance of success, and so on.  You cannot end an encounter until you make one of the choices presented.

Your odds of success or failure will be based on your character's abilities, and situation bonuses, which you can see by typing STATUS. Your abilities: Might, Talent, Seacraft, Shadowcraft, and Dreamcraft, represent skills your character possesses. Situation bonuses, represented by symbols on your status screen, give you bonuses to encounters of a certain genre or type. For example, a +10 bonus to [!] will give your character a greater chance of success for all options in encouners marked with the [!] symbol, which tend to be encounters representing action or adventure scenarios: traps, dragons, explosions, and the like.

Your character's ability scores or situation bonuses are determined by the options you select at the start of the game, and by the items you choose to EQUIP or WEAR as the game goes on.

Unlike most role playing games, you have no health, no hit points, and no lives. You cannot die. Instead, failures will give you Unpleasance (which decreases your final score), while success will generally give you pleasance, items, cargo, or other rewards.

As an open world game, there is no final goal or overarching story: do whatever you want! You can end the game at any time by typing WAKE UP.

There is much more to the game, which you can discover if you read the manual, or play the tutorial.

[~] ENCOUNTER: THE SEA WITCH

The warrior waits in the center of town, an impossibly tall man, with impossibly broad shoulders. He leans on a sheathed sword that is somehow taller and broader than he is.

Age has weathered his mighty frame only a little. His flowing beard has a few specks of red. His face is a mass of scars and tattoos of a thousand scattered tribes.

He hails you as you approach. Somehow, he was expecting you.

"Hark dreamer!" He calls. "I am perhaps the greatest warrior in the whole of the Dream. There is little upon the world, or under the sea, that I could not kill...but old age has fallen upon me. The time has come to hang up this mighty sword, but there are still a handful of horrors I have not dared to face on my own. With your help, I can slay a few more monsters, and rest. You will come with me, of course."

...and he speaks the truth, for how could you refuse such a challenge. He squats in the dirt, and draws out a map to your first challenge.

"In the Wild Blue Yonder beyond the Tropic of Zael, there waits a fearsome-sea witch, the terror of the darker oceans of the dream. Many men have sailed forth to chop off her sea-green head, but none have succeeded...we shall be the first. At dawn, we shall sail for her haunt, and you will assist me in laying her low. A word of caution: keep your wits about you! The legends say she is so beautiful to look upon that many brave souls fall instantly under her spell, only to be eaten alive...so I guess we'll have to fight blindfolded. No biggie!"

He slaps you on the shoulder, and indeed, the two of you depart at dawn.

Your voyage North is fraught with peril, of course, but the thousand lesser terrors you face along the way are scarecely worth mentioning. One day, out there in the Wild Blue Yonder, a call from the crows nest rings out: "Sea witch sighted!"

With a mighty yalwp, the warrior dons his blindfold, takes up his sword, and leaps (fully armored) into the sea.

...and the sea witch rises from the waves. The legends were completely false, for she is hideous to look upon: ten thousand tentacles erupt from her bulbous body. Her hands are ragged claws the size of your sails. Her hair is stringy and kinked with kelp and cockle-shells.
