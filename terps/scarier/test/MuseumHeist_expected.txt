   Museum Heist

You are Gentleman Finn, the elegant art thief. You have never been caught and you are widely known among criminals. Today will be your hardest job so far. You will have to rappel down through a skylight into the sculpture collection of the finest art museum in the world. From there you must immediately go west, then north to the most valuable collection of paintings in the world. Here you must steal the Mon Alicia which was painted in 1512 by the Italian artist Leonardo Avicii. The alarm will go off. That is inevitable. Then you will have ten minutes to steal something more before the police arrives. To escape from the museum you must go back to the the sculpture collection, attach the dangling rope to yourself again and push the red button on your watch. Your apprentice Anna will then receive a signal and she will activate a mechanism which pulls the rope with you and your stolen treasures up through the skylight. Make sure that you have attached the rope to yourself before pushing the red button!

Let's hope that everything goes as planned.

It is time. You have just cut open the skylight with an advanced tool so that the alarm does not go off. It's a long way down but you quickly rappel down to the floor. You detach yourself from the rope and leave it hanging. You quickly make your way towards the Mon Alicia, which is protected by bulletproof glass. Still the museum staff needs access to the painting. The glass enclosure can be unlocked with a very special key, which you just happen to have a copy of (long story). You unlock the glass enclosure and the alarm goes off. You ignore the noise, knowing that you only have ten minutes before the police arrives. You cut the painting off the frame with your utility knife (part of your multi-tool), roll up the painting and put it in your lightweight tube carrier, which is attached to your big backpack. That was the most valuable treasure. Still, a lot of other valuable art exhibits can be found in the museum. You might as well go look for those which are both valuable and portable.

Etruscan Collection
The Etruscan civilization was a civilization of ancient Italy, which developed about 900 BC until its assimilation into the Roman society about 400 BC. Though Etruscan sculptures in cast bronze were widely exported at that time, only few large examples exist today as the material was too valuable and was therefore recycled. Thus the surviving examples are of great value.

The highlight of this collection is the Capital Wolf, a bronze sculpture which isn't very big but very well-kept.  Exits are north, east and west.

> e

Egyptian Collection
You are walking among many thousand Egyptian antiquities dating from 4000 BC to the 4th century AD. Normally you would enjoy looking at these objects for several hours, but today you are in a hurry.

In the center of this huge room is the impressive Corvette Stone with both hieroglyphic and Demotic scripts. Luckily it isn't as big as the more famous Rosetta Stone - you feel that you have a chance of stealing this heavy artifact. You estimate the stone to weigh about hundred kilograms.
