The Book of Jax

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it. The game is also quite long and there is the possibility of sudden death.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game may not be for you.

I do hope, however, that you will give it a try! A hint sheet is packaged with the game, and a walkthrough is available on request (type HELP).

Many thanks.

Larry Horsfield

The Shady Glade
You are towing the cart in a pleasant, shady glade in the forest. The grass is dotted with colourful flowers and bushes and a neat thatched cottage stands in the very centre. White smoke drifts lazily into the air from a crooked chimney on top of the thatched roof. You can go South to the paved path that bought you here or In to the cottage.

As you tow the cart into the shady glade, you see that Jax is waiting for you by the door of her cottage.

"Welcome back, Duke Alaric," she greets you. "I see you were successful, well done!"

She walks over to the jars and puts her hands on the vibrating jars. "My, my, you did get a lot!" she exclaims.

"It will take me some time to extract the venom from the bees - don't worry, they won't be harmed - so you might as well take it easy until I have completed my work."

She waves a hand nonchalantly at you and suddenly you find yourself removing the harness and walking into Jax's cottage. You sit yourself on a sofa and go to sleep...

You wake up an unknown number of hours later to see Jax standing in front of you holding one of the pottery jars and a sheet of parchment.

"Welcome back to the land of the living, Duke Alaric," she greets you, as you stand up.

"In this jar is enough of the bee venom, when added to the other ingredients, to produce enough of the cure to treat 10,000 people, which I think will be more than enough to cover the population of Hecate," she tells you. "Especially as it only takes one drop per person to cure them."

She hands you the jar and the parchment. "Take great care of this jar, Duke Alaric, the survival of Hecate depends on its contents. It will be safe in your bag, do not take it out at any time, until you reach Magor."

She waves her hand and the jar and parchment disappear from view.

"Farewell, Duke Alaric, it was very nice meeting you. I can transport you to where you have left your horse, so do not be worried about what is about to happen."

She waves her hand and the scene in front of you dissolves into spots and whirls for a second or two before resolving again and you see you are in a different place....
