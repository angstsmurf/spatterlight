Die Feuerfaust

A Note For The Player:

This adventure game is very much "old school" in that the gameplay involves having to SEARCH objects and/or LOOK BEHIND, IN or UNDER objects to find objects that you will need during the game. You might also have to EXAMINE WALL to find something on it.

If you do not like this style of adventure gameplay then regrettably this game is not for you.

Many thanks

Larry Horsfield

Start Options:

If you have already started the game and wish to restart from a saved position, click on the blue folder icon above.

To Begin the game from the first location, press B and then Enter.

To read the prologue and playing instructions (highly recommended if you  are starting this game for the first time) press P and then Enter.

PLEASE NOTE: This game was envisioned as being playable Cyan On Black. Since ADRIFT does not yet support an authorial override of a player's background colour, be advised that if your background colour is not black, portions of this game will not be easily readable. For maximum experience as intended by the author please ensure your background colour is set to BLACK.

If you are visually impaired you can increase or decrease the font size whilst playing this game by holding down the CTRL key and moving your mouse wheel.

Please ensure that Autocomplete is unticked in the "Edit" tab  above.

Backereigasse
You are in Backereigasse, a busy street with people walking along it, north of the town square. There is a bakery on the west side, and opposite that is the rickety house where Stiggy lives. An open door leads East inside the house.

As you enter Backereigasse, you notice a small boy sitting by the wall of a building. At first you assume he is a beggar but he looks too well dressed. Then you see that he is sitting by a small hole in the base of the wooden wall of the building, staring at it intently. Fascinated, you stop to see what he is doing...

You see him put a small piece of cheese in the hole, which you realise is a mouse hole. He then puts another piece of cheese an inch or two from the first and then a couple more, each a bit further away until the last piece is within his reach.

After a while, a mouse pokes its nose out of the hole and grabs the cheese, disappearing back into the hole like a flash. A minute or two later, it dashes out and grabs the next piece of cheese. This is repeated, each time the mouse venturing further out of the hole until it goes to grab the last piece whereupon, the boy's hands dart out and he captures the mouse.

With a cry of "Yes!", he gets to his feet and scampers off along the alley, leaving you scratching your head and wondering what the hell that was all about!

In Stiggy's House
You're in Stiggy's house in the north part of Sagard. On a workbench against the east wall is a crazy contraption of pipes, tubes and glass vessels that hisses, bubbles and seethes away noisily. Stiggy himself stands by the bench, watching the process carefully and making adjustments when necessary. A door leads West to the street outside but you notice the other walls are completely blank.

As you enter the room, your gaze is drawn to the contraption on the table, which Stiggy is adjusting.

"I'll be with you in a second or two,"he says to you without taking his eyes off the thing. "Crucial stage in the process, you understand?"

You don't, but you give him the benefit of the doubt and wait patiently. After a minute or two he visibly relaxes, but keeps his eyes on the apparatus.

"Please, is there anything I can give you in return?" he asks you.

> ask stiggy for light
You ask Stiggy if he has a light you can have.

The little old man smiles and gestures towards a pile of objects in the corner of the room, muttering under his breath. To your amazement the pile starts shifting of its own volition and an object emerges. The object then flies across the room into Stiggy's outstretched hand. It is a length of wood about as long as a normal pitch torch, but with a lump of white resin-like material on one end.

"This is a magic torch I invented recently," he tells you. "To light it you use the spell TASCH ZUNDE," - upon which the lump of resin starts to glow with a bright white light - "And to extinguish it you use the spell TASCH LOSCH." The light fades away very quickly.

Stiggy hands you the torch and you feel the lump of resin, which is completely cold. You thank Stiggy for his marvellous gift.

"I'm...err...not sure how long the spell will last, though," he tells you. "Haven't had time to test it, so make sure you turn it off when you are finished with it."
